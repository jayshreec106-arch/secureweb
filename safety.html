Preventive Defense

Essential Cyber Safety Tips

Establishing clean digital habits can prevent the vast majority of online security breaches. Follow these guidelines to stay protected.

1. Never Share OTPs

An OTP (One-Time Password) is the final guard verifying transactions or account logins. Sharing it is equivalent to handing over the keys to your vault.

No bank or service provider will ever call to ask you for an OTP.

2. Use Strong Passwords

Avoid passwords containing dictionary words, birthdates, or sequential numbers. Use a mix of uppercase, lowercase, special characters, and digits.

Make passphrase sentences like "BlUe!SkY#77_RaIn" which are tough for bots to crack.

3. Enable 2-Factor Auth (2FA)

2FA requires an additional authentication step (e.g. mobile authenticator app prompt) along with your regular password, adding an secondary security layer.

Blocks up to 99% of automated credential theft attempts.

4. Avoid Suspicious Links

Always hover over URLs to inspect their true destination. Scammers disguise links to lead to malicious cloning pages.

If you receive SMS or WhatsApp deals with weird links, delete them.

5. Keep Software Updated

OS and app updates patch critical security vulnerabilities that hackers exploit. Keep your computer and phone software updated regularly.

Enable automatic updates on your devices for hassle-free protection.

6. Regular Backup Data

Keep backup records of important pictures, document files, and university assignments on offline hard drives or secure cloud databases.

Saves your files in the event of malware or ransomware attacks.

Spotted a Cyber Crime or Scam?

If you have experienced or witnessed digital fraud, reporting it immediately can prevent others from falling into the same trap. You can report it here on our local reporting page, or officially file a complaint at the National Cyber Crime Portal.
