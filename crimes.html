Phishing Scams

Critical Threat
What It Is

Phishing is a method where attackers send deceptive communications (mostly emails, SMS, or direct messages) designed to look like they come from a reputable, trusted organization (e.g., bank, Netflix, college portals, government agencies).

How It Works
  1. Deceptive Delivery: You receive an email stating your account will be suspended unless you click a link to verify credentials.
  2. The Trap: The link takes you to a fake website that looks identical to the official login page.
  3. Data Capture: When you enter your username, password, or credit card details, they are sent directly to the scammer.
Common Phishing Clues:
  • Generic greeting: "Dear Customer" instead of your name.
  • Slightly misspelled email domain (e.g., info@netf1ix.com).
  • Urgent call to action (e.g., "Act within 24 hours or face suspension").
Prevention Tips
  • Never click links in unsolicited emails or SMS. Instead, type the official website address directly into your browser.
  • Double-check the sender's actual email address, not just the display name.
  • Use two-factor authentication (2FA) so that password theft alone isn't enough to compromise your account.

UPI Fraud

High Threat
What It Is

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud occurs when cybercriminals exploit digital payment systems to steal money from victims' bank accounts. In most cases, scammers exploit users' lack of technical understanding regarding how UPI transactions function.

How It Works
  1. Fake Support/Buyer: Scammers pose as customer support agents, bank executives, or online buyers on sites like OLX.
  2. Request Money Trap: The scammer initiates a "Request Money" transaction through a UPI app and asks the victim to authorize it.
  3. PIN input: The victim enters their UPI PIN thinking they are *receiving* money, but this actually transfers the money to the scammer.
Golden Rule of UPI:

UPI PIN is only required to SEND/DEBIT money, never to receive it. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to receive funds, they are trying to rob you.

Prevention Tips
  • Never share your UPI PIN or banking passwords with anyone under any circumstances.
  • Do not click on UPI request notifications from unknown callers or marketplace buyers.
  • Always read the transaction screen message carefully before entering your PIN.

QR Code Scams

Medium Threat
What It Is

A QR (Quick Response) code scam involves malicious barcodes designed to direct users to phishing sites, initiate fraudulent UPI payment authorizations, or install silent malware applications on the scanned mobile device.

How It Works
  1. Bait Offer: Scammers send QR codes via WhatsApp or email, claiming it will credit cashback, lucky draw prizes, or payment for a product you are selling.
  2. The Scan: You scan the code using a scanner or UPI app.
  3. The Action: The scanned code either redirects you to a web page asking for payment confirmation, or triggers a payment debit from your account.
Prevention Tips
  • Never scan a QR code if you are expecting to receive money.
  • Avoid scanning printed QR codes pasted randomly on public spaces unless verified.
  • When scanning, check the destination URL displayed on your screen before proceeding.

Social Media Scams

High Threat
What It Is

Social media scams leverage identity theft, profile cloning, fake lottery alerts, or deceptive advertising on popular platforms like Instagram, Facebook, and Telegram to exploit trust and steal financial assets.

How It Works
  1. Profile Cloning: Attackers copy pictures and details of your close friend to create a fake clone account.
  2. Emergency Plea: The clone profile messages you, claiming an emergency (medical accident, stuck somewhere) and asks for immediate money transfer.
  3. Impersonation Sweep: Once you transfer the money, they block your account.
Prevention Tips
  • If a friend asks for money on social media, call them directly on their cellular number to verify first.
  • Never share secondary login codes (OTP, reset links) sent to your phone. Scammers use these to hijack accounts.
  • Set your profiles to private and restrict strangers from viewing your friend lists.

Online Shopping Fraud

Medium Threat
What It Is

This fraud involves dummy websites claiming to sell premium electronics, clothing, or products at heavily discounted rates. They accept payments but never deliver the products, or send sub-standard fake items.

How It Works
  1. Advert Baiting: Scammers place alluring ads on Instagram or Facebook showcasing high-value products at 80% discounts.
  2. Checkout & Pay: You click the link, register on the clean-looking website, and pay via UPI or credit card.
  3. Ghosting: The site disappears, customer support email bounces back, and no product is shipped.
Prevention Tips
  • Shop only from recognized, verified brands or trusted marketplaces.
  • Be cautious of prices that seem too good to be true—they almost always are fake.
  • Look for customer reviews on external sites and verify physical addresses/contact details.
